About

Project Overview

The IMT PC Hardware Servicing Website is an instructional media toolkit developed to enhance the teaching and learning process in the field of Information and Communications Technology (ICT). This platform was conceptualized as part of an academic thesis initiative aimed at addressing the challenges encountered in traditional PC Hardware Servicing instruction.

Rationale of the Study

In many educational institutions, the delivery of PC Hardware Servicing lessons is limited by insufficient laboratory equipment, outdated hardware components, time constraints, and the potential risk of damage during hands-on activities. These limitations restrict students’ opportunities to repeatedly practice computer assembly and component identification.

To address these concerns, this project introduces a digital, component-based instructional platform that allows learners to explore, understand, and review computer hardware concepts in a structured and accessible manner. The website serves as a supplementary learning tool designed to reinforce theoretical knowledge and prepare students for practical laboratory activities.

Statement of the Problem

This project seeks to answer the following educational concerns:

  • How can instructional delivery in PC Hardware Servicing be improved through digital integration?
  • How can students strengthen their conceptual understanding of computer components before hands-on application?
  • How can interactive web-based tools enhance student engagement and knowledge retention?

Objectives of the Project

  • To develop a structured digital learning platform for PC Hardware Servicing.
  • To provide accessible and organized information about computer components and peripherals.
  • To integrate interactive elements such as quizzes for knowledge reinforcement.
  • To support competency-based technical education aligned with curriculum standards.

Educational Contribution

This website contributes to ICT education by promoting blended learning, where digital tools complement traditional hands-on instruction. By allowing students to review lessons outside the laboratory environment, the platform strengthens independent learning, increases familiarity with hardware components, and builds learner confidence.

The integration of structured modules, component categorization, and formative assessments supports a learner-centered approach that enhances both comprehension and retention.

Scope and Limitations

The website focuses primarily on the identification, description, and functional understanding of internal PC components and basic peripherals. While it includes interactive quizzes for reinforcement, it does not replace actual laboratory experience. Instead, it serves as a preparatory and supplementary instructional tool.

Future Development

Future improvements may include the integration of a virtual PC assembly simulator, performance tracking system, expanded assessment modules, and enhanced multimedia resources to further enrich the learning experience.
